3D Printing in New Orleans

The New Orleans metro is home to major offshore oil & gas operators and suppliers whose equipment needs precision-manufactured replacement parts — increasingly produced via metal and engineering-grade 3D printing.

The city's unique architectural heritage creates demand for historic restoration work, custom architectural details, and preservation components. Meanwhile, its food and hospitality industries use 3D printing for everything from custom restaurant equipment to props and event installations.

Industries We Serve in New Orleans

Oil & Gas Equipment

Offshore operators and Gulf Coast energy suppliers use 3D printing to produce critical replacement parts and reduce equipment downtime.

Historic Preservation & Architecture

New Orleans' preservation community uses 3D printing to reproduce historic architectural details, decorative ironwork, and building components.

Food & Hospitality

The city's world-class restaurant and hospitality industry uses 3D printing for custom kitchen equipment, branded items, and event installations.

Defense & Government

NAS JRB New Orleans and nearby defense installations drive demand for rapid part production and specialized military equipment support.

Marine & Port Operations

The Port of New Orleans and marine industry use 3D printing for custom vessel hardware, dock equipment, and maritime component manufacturing.

Creative & Event Production

New Orleans' vibrant entertainment scene uses 3D printing for Mardi Gras float components, stage props, custom costumes, and event installations.
