Best 3D Printing Services in Austin, TX (2026)

7 min read Austin, TX

Austin has become one of the most vibrant tech and maker cities in the United States. With a thriving startup scene, a massive UT campus, and an ever-growing manufacturing base, demand for 3D printing services in Austin is higher than ever. Whether you need a quick prototype, production-quality nylon parts, or a full-service design bureau, Austin has you covered.

This guide covers the types of 3D printing services available in Austin, what to look for when choosing a provider, and how to find the right shop for your specific project.

Types of 3D Printing Services in Austin

FDM Print Shops

FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling) is the most widely available 3D printing technology in Austin. Dozens of small and medium-sized shops offer FDM printing in PLA, PETG, ABS, TPU, and engineering materials like Nylon and Polycarbonate. FDM is ideal for functional prototypes, custom enclosures, brackets, and anything where speed and low cost matter more than surface finish.

Most FDM shops in Austin offer same-day or next-day turnaround on small parts. Many accept STL files directly via email or their website, with instant quoting available at some locations.

SLA / Resin Printing

SLA (Stereolithography) and MSLA resin printing is available at several Austin studios and professional service bureaus. Resin printing produces dramatically smoother surfaces and finer detail than FDM — it's the right choice for dental models, jewelry, miniatures, and prototype housings where presentation matters.

Austin's dental and medical industry has driven strong demand for professional resin printing, so you'll find several labs offering biocompatible and castable resins alongside standard options.

SLS and Professional Bureaus

For engineering-grade nylon parts, Austin has several professional service bureaus offering SLS (Selective Laser Sintering) printing. SLS produces strong, isotropic nylon parts without support structures — ideal for complex assemblies, functional testing, and small production runs. Expect lead times of 3–7 days and higher per-part costs than FDM.

Makerspaces with Open Access

Austin has a strong makerspace culture. Several community spaces offer FDM printing access for a small per-hour or per-gram fee, or as part of a monthly membership. This is the best option if you want to learn to print yourself, or if you have a very small print that doesn't justify a full service bureau quote.

Pro Tip

Austin's tech scene means most professional 3D printing shops are accustomed to serving engineers and product designers — they'll review your files for printability and suggest material options. Don't be afraid to ask for advice before committing to an order.

What to Look for When Choosing an Austin 3D Printing Service

Technology Match

Make sure the shop offers the technology you need. An FDM shop can't help you with a jewelry casting pattern that requires castable resin. Check the shop's website or call ahead to confirm they support your required technology and material before sending files.

Turnaround Time

Austin has many shops that offer same-day or next-day FDM printing for small parts. For SLA and SLS, expect 2–5 days. For metal printing, you'll likely be working with a specialized bureau with 1–2 week lead times. If you're on a deadline, ask about rush options — many Austin shops offer expedited service for an additional fee.

File Preparation

Most Austin shops accept STL, OBJ, or STEP files. If you're not a designer, some shops offer in-house CAD and design services — useful if you have a concept or a physical part you need replicated. Make sure your model is watertight (no holes or non-manifold geometry) before submitting.

Pricing

FDM printing in Austin typically runs $10–$80 for small to medium parts in standard materials. SLA starts around $20–$40 for small parts. SLS is significantly more expensive — expect $50–$300+ per part depending on size and complexity. Get quotes from 2–3 shops for larger orders.
