Best 3D Printing Services in Seattle (2026)

7 min read Seattle, WA

Seattle is home to one of the most technically sophisticated 3D printing markets in the United States. Driven by Boeing's aerospace supply chain, Amazon and Microsoft's product innovation labs, and a thriving startup ecosystem, the Puget Sound region has world-class 3D printing services across every technology and material category.

This guide covers where to find 3D printing services around Seattle, which industries they serve, and how to choose the right shop for your project.

The Seattle 3D Printing Landscape

Seattle's 3D printing services cluster around the region's major industries:

  • South Lake Union / Capitol Hill — tech company prototyping, startup product design, consumer electronics
  • SODO / Georgetown — industrial fabrication, manufacturing support, large-format printing
  • Bellevue / Redmond / Kirkland — tech corporate shops (Microsoft, Nintendo of America), consumer product prototyping
  • Renton / Kent / Auburn — Boeing supply chain, aerospace manufacturing, metal printing
  • Everett — aerospace (Boeing Everett), heavy manufacturing

FDM Printing in Seattle

FDM printing is available throughout the Seattle metro, from Capitol Hill maker studios to Kent industrial shops. Tech companies on the Eastside (Redmond, Bellevue) are major FDM consumers — many use local print services for rapid product iteration. Consumer electronics prototyping, hardware startup development, and IoT device housing fabrication are common FDM applications in the Seattle market.

For large-format FDM, SODO and Georgetown industrial shops can handle oversized parts — boat components, outdoor equipment, architectural pieces. Seattle's maker culture also means strong community FDM infrastructure through the Makerhaus and similar spaces.

SLA / Resin Printing in Seattle

High-resolution resin printing in Seattle primarily serves the tech product design, dental, and jewelry markets. South Lake Union and Capitol Hill shops offer professional SLA for startups that need photorealistic prototypes for product launches and investor demos. Amazon's internal hardware teams and countless hardware startups in the SLU neighborhood have built strong demand for premium SLA services with tight turnaround expectations.

Boeing Supply Chain Note

Boeing's presence in Renton and Everett has spawned a dense network of aerospace suppliers and fabrication shops throughout the south King County corridor. Many of these shops have 3D printing capabilities — including high-performance polymer and metal printing — used for tooling, jigs, fixtures, and non-flight prototype parts.

Aerospace and Metal Printing Near Seattle

The Renton-to-Everett corridor along I-405 and SR-99 hosts Seattle's most serious industrial 3D printing capacity. Boeing and its supply chain partners have driven adoption of metal DMLS and SLM printing for tooling, end-use aerospace parts, and rapid prototyping of flight hardware alternatives. Several shops in this corridor hold AS9100 and Nadcap certifications.

For non-aerospace metal printing (medical devices, consumer products, industrial equipment), shops in the Kent manufacturing district offer DMLS in steel, titanium, and aluminum with standard commercial lead times of 1–2 weeks.

Makerspaces and Community Access

Seattle has a strong maker culture. Makerhaus in South Lake Union offers member access to FDM printers, laser cutters, CNC mills, and other fabrication equipment. The Seattle Public Library's Makerspace in the Central Library location has free 3D printing access for cardholders. University of Washington's engineering school labs are available to students and some community partners.

Tips for Ordering 3D Prints in Seattle

  • Tech-market expectations: Seattle shops serving tech companies move fast. If you need a quick quote and turnaround, expect most shops to have online ordering or at minimum fast email response.
  • Traffic and geography: The I-5 and 520 corridors can be brutal during rush hour. If your shop is on the Eastside and you're in Seattle proper, factor in 45–90 minute travel times for pickup. Many shops offer local delivery.
  • Amazon and Microsoft prototyping: Some shops near Redmond specialize in serving large tech clients and may have minimum order requirements. Check before ordering a single-piece prototype.
  • Rain and humidity: Not your concern — FDM filament moisture sensitivity is handled inside climate-controlled shops. But if you're printing outdoors or in an unheated space yourself, buy dry filament.
