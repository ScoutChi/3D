Best 3D Printing Services in Los Angeles (2026)

7 min read Los Angeles, CA

Los Angeles is home to one of the most diverse 3D printing ecosystems in the United States. From Hollywood prop shops printing on-demand set pieces to aerospace-grade metal printing facilities in the South Bay, LA has a 3D printing service for virtually every application and budget.

This guide covers what types of 3D printing services are available in Los Angeles, where to find them, and how to choose the right one for your project.

The LA 3D Printing Landscape

The Los Angeles metro area spans a huge geographic footprint — from Ventura County in the north to Long Beach in the south, and the Inland Empire to the east. 3D printing services are distributed across this area, with concentrations in:

  • Hollywood / West Hollywood — prop shops, entertainment industry fabrication, custom models
  • Culver City / West LA — design studios, architecture firms, tech companies
  • El Segundo / Torrance / South Bay — aerospace suppliers, engineering firms, metal printing
  • Downtown LA / Arts District — makerspaces, community fabrication labs
  • Burbank / Glendale — entertainment production, automotive, prototyping

FDM Printing in Los Angeles

FDM is the most widely available 3D printing technology in LA. Dozens of shops across the metro offer fast, affordable FDM printing in PLA, PETG, ABS, and engineering materials. Many offer online file upload and automatic quoting — you can have parts shipped to you without ever leaving home.

For entertainment industry work, FDM shops near Burbank and Hollywood often specialize in large-format printing for props, set pieces, and costume components. These shops typically have printers with large build volumes (300–600mm+) and experience with post-processing including painting and finishing.

SLA / Resin Printing in Los Angeles

LA's entertainment, jewelry, and dental industries create strong demand for high-detail resin printing. Several studios in West LA, Culver City, and the San Fernando Valley offer professional SLA printing with next-day turnaround.

If you're in the jewelry industry, look for shops offering castable resin — a specialized photopolymer that burns out cleanly for lost-wax casting. Several jewelry districts in downtown LA and the San Gabriel Valley have shops that specialize in this.

Entertainment Industry Note

LA print shops that serve the entertainment industry often have experience with unusual materials, large formats, and tight deadlines. They're worth considering for any complex or unusual project — even if you're not in film or TV.

Metal 3D Printing in Los Angeles

The South Bay — home to major aerospace companies including SpaceX, Northrop Grumman, and Raytheon — has several professional metal 3D printing service bureaus. These shops offer DMLS and SLM printing in titanium, stainless steel, Inconel, and aluminum alloys.

Most metal printing in LA is geared toward aerospace and defense — expect rigorous material certifications, AS9100 quality standards, and longer quoting processes than consumer-facing shops. Lead times of 1–3 weeks are typical. Find metal printing services in the LA area via the 3DPrintMap metal directory.

Makerspaces and Community Access

LA has several makerspaces offering member access to 3D printers at low per-hour rates. These are ideal for hobbyists, students, and anyone who wants to learn to print or run a quick test without committing to a full service order. Look for makerspaces in the Arts District, Culver City, and Pasadena.

Tips for Ordering 3D Prints in LA

  • Traffic matters: Pickup in LA can be a 45-minute drive that turns into 2 hours. Check if the shop ships — many do, even locally.
  • Film industry rush: If you need parts urgently for a production, say so upfront. LA shops are used to impossible deadlines and often have premium rush options.
  • Post-processing: Many LA shops offer painting, priming, sanding, and finishing in-house — valuable if you're making props or consumer-facing prototypes.
  • Volume discounts: For production runs, always ask about volume pricing. Most shops offer meaningful discounts at 10+ units.
