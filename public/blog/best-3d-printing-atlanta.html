Best 3D Printing Services in Atlanta (2026)

7 min read Atlanta, GA

Atlanta has emerged as one of the Southeast's premier 3D printing markets. Fueled by a booming tech sector, Georgia's expanding aerospace and automotive supply chains, a major film production industry, and a growing biotech community, Atlanta's 3D printing services span everything from rapid consumer prototypes to aerospace-grade metal fabrication — with over 37 services in the metro area alone.

This guide covers the types of 3D printing services available across the Atlanta metro area, where to find them, and what to expect when ordering professionally or locally.

The Atlanta 3D Printing Landscape

Atlanta's 3D printing ecosystem reflects its diverse and rapidly growing economy. Services cluster by industry and geography:

  • Midtown / Tech Square — Georgia Tech spinoffs, startup prototyping, game and film industry props, consumer electronics
  • Buckhead / Perimeter Center — corporate prototyping, marketing and display fabrication, product design agencies
  • Marietta / Kennesaw — aerospace suppliers, Lockheed Martin ecosystem, industrial manufacturing
  • Peachtree City / Newnan — automotive and tier-1 supplier prototyping (Kia, Porsche North America)
  • Downtown / Westside — film and entertainment fabrication, set prop manufacturing, creative studios

FDM Printing in Atlanta

FDM is the backbone of Atlanta's accessible 3D printing market. Shops in Midtown and around Georgia Tech offer online ordering with fast turnaround on PLA, PETG, ABS, and engineering-grade materials. Atlanta's growing startup scene means many shops are used to rapid iteration cycles and can turn around multiple prototype revisions in a single week.

Atlanta's film industry creates unique demand for large-format FDM — movie set pieces, display props, and costume components. Shops in the Downtown and Westside areas regularly handle oversized FDM builds for Atlanta's thriving production studios.

SLA / Resin Printing in Atlanta

High-resolution resin printing in Atlanta is driven by the dental and medical device sectors (particularly around Emory University Hospital and Northside Hospital's medical corridor), consumer product design agencies, and the film industry's demand for highly detailed prop work. Midtown shops serve design agencies; suburban shops in Marietta and Duluth handle more industrial biocompatible resin applications.

Film & Entertainment Note

Atlanta is one of the largest film production hubs in the United States — home to major studios and a constant stream of Marvel, Disney, and Warner Bros. productions. 3D printing shops in the Westside and Doraville areas have extensive experience with entertainment industry prop work, including painted and finished display pieces, hero prop replicas, and large-scale set dressing components.

SLS and Industrial Printing in Atlanta

SLS nylon and Multi-Jet Fusion printing serve Atlanta's strong automotive and aerospace manufacturing base. Georgia's role as a major automotive production state — with Kia in West Point, Rivian in Normal, and Porsche North America HQ in Atlanta — creates steady demand for functional prototype parts, jigs, and tooling fixtures. Service bureaus in Marietta, Peachtree City, and the airport industrial corridor near Hartsfield-Jackson serve this market.

Metal 3D Printing in Atlanta

Metal 3D printing in the Atlanta area is primarily driven by the aerospace supply chain centered on Marietta (Lockheed Martin), Bell Aircraft, and the broader Georgia aerospace ecosystem. DMLS and Direct Energy Deposition (DED) services are available from specialized shops. Expect formal quoting, engineering drawing requirements, and 1–2 week lead times. Titanium, stainless steel, and aluminum alloys are standard offerings.

Film & Entertainment?

Downtown and Westside shops have deep experience with entertainment industry prop and set piece fabrication

Aerospace & Automotive?

Marietta and Peachtree City serve the Lockheed, Kia, and Porsche supply chains with industrial-grade printing

Makerspaces and Community Printing in Atlanta

Atlanta has a growing maker community. Freeside Atlanta is one of the city's oldest and largest makerspaces, offering member access to 3D printers, CNC machines, laser cutters, and electronics labs. Georgia Tech's Invention Studio is one of the largest student-run makerspaces in the US and offers community access through structured programs. The Atlanta Public Library system has introduced 3D printers at several branches. Additionally, Chattahoochee Hills creative districts host maker communities with shared printing access.

Tips for Ordering 3D Prints in Atlanta

  • Film industry rush: Atlanta production schedules are aggressive — if you're ordering for film or TV, be upfront about your shoot date. Many shops have experience with entertainment industry rush orders.
  • Aerospace compliance: For Lockheed, Gulfstream, or other aerospace programs, confirm whether the shop has AS9100 or NADCAP certifications if your part is for flight or program use.
  • Georgia Tech resources: If you're affiliated with Georgia Tech, the Invention Studio and CREATE-X accelerator provide subsidized prototyping resources for students and startup teams.
  • Metro geography: Atlanta's metro is large — factor in delivery logistics when choosing a shop in Marietta vs. Midtown vs. the Airport corridor.
