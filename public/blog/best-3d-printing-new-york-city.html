Best 3D Printing Services in New York City (2026)

7 min read New York, NY

New York City has one of the densest concentrations of 3D printing services in the United States. From Brooklyn's thriving maker community to Midtown Manhattan's product design studios, NYC offers professional 3D printing for every application — architecture models, fashion accessories, medical prototypes, theatrical props, and full production runs.

This guide covers the types of 3D printing services available across the five boroughs, where to find them, and what to expect when ordering.

The NYC 3D Printing Landscape

3D printing services in New York are distributed across the city, with distinct clusters tied to industry concentration:

  • Midtown Manhattan — product design agencies, architecture firms, medical device prototyping
  • Brooklyn (Dumbo, Bushwick, Industry City) — maker studios, artist fabrication, small-batch production
  • Lower Manhattan / SoHo — fashion accessories, jewelry, consumer product startups
  • Long Island City, Queens — industrial shops, engineering services
  • Garment District — fashion tech, textile-adjacent prototyping

FDM Printing in NYC

FDM is the most accessible and affordable option in New York City. Dozens of service bureaus and print-on-demand shops offer fast turnaround on PLA, PETG, ABS, and engineering-grade materials. Most Manhattan and Brooklyn shops offer online ordering with city-wide delivery or courier pickup.

For larger projects — architectural scale models, theatrical set pieces, large prop fabrication — look for Brooklyn-based shops with industrial-scale printers. Industry City in Sunset Park and similar creative manufacturing hubs host several shops with large-format FDM capability (400mm+ build volumes).

SLA / Resin Printing in NYC

High-resolution resin printing is widely available in NYC, driven by demand from the jewelry, dental, fashion, and consumer product design industries. SLA shops concentrated in SoHo, Midtown, and the Flatiron district serve product designers and agencies that need prototype-quality surface finish on short timelines.

NYC jewelry designers frequently use castable resin services — photopolymer resins that burn out cleanly for lost-wax casting. If you're in the jewelry trade, ask specifically for castable or wax-pattern resin, not standard photopolymer.

Architecture & Design Note

NYC's architecture and interior design firms are heavy consumers of 3D printing. Many Midtown and Flatiron shops specialize in white architectural models, concept massing models, and presentation-quality pieces. If you're an architect, these shops understand your file formats (Rhino, Revit exports) and delivery expectations.

SLS and Industrial Printing in NYC

SLS nylon printing — ideal for functional, snap-fit, and moving parts — is available from several service bureaus serving NYC's medical device, consumer electronics, and industrial product design sectors. These shops are often not walk-in; expect online quoting and 3–7 day lead times.

Multi-Jet Fusion (MJF) printing, which produces stronger parts than standard SLS, is available from larger shops and online services that ship to NYC same-day or next-day. For short-run production of functional nylon parts, MJF is often the best value.

Metal 3D Printing in NYC

Metal 3D printing in NYC is primarily served by service bureaus with national reach — most accept online orders and ship finished parts. For locally-produced metal printing, look for shops in Long Island City and New Jersey's Hudson County that serve the city's engineering and manufacturing sectors. Expect lead times of 1–2 weeks and rigorous quoting for metal parts.

Need it overnight?

Several Midtown shops offer same-day FDM and next-day SLA for rush product design work

Need high detail?

SoHo and Flatiron resin shops serve jewelry, fashion, and consumer product designers

Makerspaces and Community Printing in NYC

For hobbyists, students, and one-off projects, NYC's makerspace scene offers affordable member access to 3D printers. NYC Resistor in Brooklyn, Hack Manhattan, and public library FabLabs offer low-cost access — some branches of the New York Public Library and Brooklyn Public Library have free-to-use 3D printers for cardholders.

Tips for Ordering 3D Prints in NYC

  • Delivery vs. pickup: Many NYC shops offer messenger or courier delivery to Manhattan — for small parts, this is faster than traveling to pick up in person.
  • Rush fees: Same-day and overnight printing commands a significant premium in NYC. Budget 2–3x the standard rate for same-day turnaround.
  • File prep: NYC design shops are experienced with most CAD formats (STEP, STL, OBJ, 3MF). Send your native file if possible — they can slice and optimize better than you can.
  • Volume pricing: For more than 10 units, always request a volume quote. NYC production shops offer significant discounts at scale.
