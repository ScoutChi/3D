3D Printing in Kansas City

Straddling two states, the Kansas City metro is one of the Midwest's most important manufacturing and logistics centers. Its 3D printing shops serve a wide range of clients — from automotive suppliers and agricultural equipment makers to construction firms and healthcare providers.

The region's growing aerospace presence, anchored by Garmin and Spirit AeroSystems proximity, plus a robust healthcare sector including the University of Kansas Health System, drives consistent demand for precision prototyping and custom parts.

Industries We Serve in Kansas City

Automotive & Transportation

Kansas City's auto assembly plants and supplier network use 3D printing for prototype parts, tooling jigs, and production fixtures throughout the supply chain.

Agriculture & Food Processing

The region's food processing and agriculture industries use additive manufacturing for custom equipment components, replacement parts, and packaging prototypes.

Healthcare & Medical

The University of Kansas Health System and regional hospitals use 3D printing for surgical planning models, medical device prototypes, and custom implant templates.

Construction & Architecture

Kansas City's active construction market uses 3D printing for architectural scale models, custom building components, and design presentation pieces.

Aerospace & Defense

Garmin and regional aerospace suppliers use 3D printing for avionics housing prototypes, tooling components, and precision-engineered parts.

Manufacturing & Industrial

Kansas City's broad industrial base uses additive manufacturing for custom tooling, jigs, fixtures, and low-volume production components across sectors.
